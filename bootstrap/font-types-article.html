Font Types Article

Fonts play a major role in how information is communicated on a webpage. Different font types convey different moods, levels of formality, and stylistic personalities. In this article, I examine several major font categories and provide examples of fonts commonly used in web design. Each example includes a sample preview and a link to download the font.

Serif Fonts

Serif fonts include small decorative strokes at the ends of letterforms. They are often associated with tradition, reliability, and professionalism.

Times New Roman — “The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.”
Download Merriweather (Google Fonts)

Sans Serif Fonts

Sans serif fonts do not have decorative strokes. They are modern, clean, and highly readable on screens.

Arial — “The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.”
Download Roboto (Google Fonts)

Script Fonts

Script fonts mimic handwriting or calligraphy. They are elegant but should be used sparingly due to reduced readability at small sizes.

Brush Script — “The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.”
Download Dancing Script (Google Fonts)

Display Fonts

Display fonts are highly stylized and best used for large headlines. They grab attention and express personality but are not suited for body text.

Impact — “The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.”
Download Bebas Neue (Google Fonts)

Monospace Fonts

Monospace fonts give every letter the same fixed width. They are commonly used for code and technical layouts.

Courier New — “The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.”
Download Source Code Pro (Google Fonts)
