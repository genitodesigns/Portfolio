Typography Overview

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type. It includes the choice of fonts, the spacing between letters and lines, the size of the text, and the overall visual structure of written content. Good typography improves readability, guides the viewer’s eye, and helps create a strong visual hierarchy throughout a design.

Typography Key Terms

  • Typeface: The overall design or family of letters (e.g., Arial).
  • Font: A specific style and size of a typeface (e.g., Arial Bold 16px).
  • Serif: Small decorative strokes at the ends of letterforms.
  • Sans Serif: Typefaces without serif strokes, offering a cleaner look.
  • Kerning: The adjustment of space between individual letters.
  • Leading: The vertical spacing between lines of text.
  • Tracking: The overall spacing applied across a range of letters.
  • Baseline: The invisible line on which text characters sit.
  • X-Height: The height of lowercase letters, typically measured by the letter "x".
  • Hierarchy: The use of size, weight, and spacing to guide the reader’s eye.
  • Weight: How thick or thin the strokes of a font appear (e.g., light, regular, bold).
  • Alignment: How text is positioned horizontally (left, right, centered, justified).
